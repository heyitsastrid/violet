There's a Spanish expression called "tailor's drawer" ([cajón de sastre](https://dle.rae.es/caj%C3%B3n#5mQvJUk)) used to refer to a messy collection of random items.

This page is precisely that, a messy collection of random stuff I have or find interesting.

## Links

I do recommend what I put in this section (at least at the time of writing).

### Public transit related

* [Ferroviària.cat](https://ferroviaria.cat/) <small>[ca]</small>, a PWA that tells you the ETA for rail transit in Catalonia (including Rodalies).
* [Trenscat.cat](https://www.trenscat.com/) <small>[ca]</small>, *the* website about trains in Catalonia.
* [Stations and transfers](http://stations.albertguillaumes.cat/) by [Albert Guillaumes](http://albertguillaumes.cat/), a collection of 3D diagrams of different rapid transit stations around the world, including most European cities.
* [Metro intervals](http://albertguillaumes.cat/intervals/) <small>[ca]</small> also by Albert Guillaumes, a website listing the intervals between each metro in every big European city.
* [RFIG map](https://drive.google.com/file/d/19Ls-C8UPkrrcdvbySHGzy5U6sbbUzUDi/), an unofficial map by [maparfig (BlueSky)](https://bsky.app/profile/maparfig.bsky.social) of the Spanish rail network (managed by ADIF).
* [Puntual.cat](https://www.puntual.cat/) <small>[ca]</small>, it lists the average delay for Rodalies trains, so you can plan your trips accordingly.
* [Ferropanel](https://ferropanel.rubenbupe.com/configurator) <small>[es]</small> by Rubén Buzón Pérez, allows you to use Adif's API to get the official screens Adif uses in their stations.
* [FGC](https://www.fgc.cat/), it's just plain better than Renfe.
* [Promoció del Transport Públic](https://transportpublic.org/) <small>[ca]</small>, a transit advocacy group.
